This class satisfies the seminar requirements within the department of visual and media arts at Emerson College. It is taught in conjunction with the Engagement Game Lab and seeks to advance the possibilities of working with media to enhance our urban environments.

VM303: Ubiquitous Media

about the class

This class will examine how mobile devices and location awareness are transforming media consumption and production, and as a consequence, transforming interpersonal communication, community organization and political boundaries in the city.  We will look at the convergence of location technologies and mobile computing over the last decade and identify the various genres that have emerged, including augmented reality, alternate reality games, location-based social networks, and location-based annotations.  The class will focus on how these genres are creating new cultural norms for communicating in public spaces, as well as opening up new opportunities for protest and organization, especially in the developing world.

The laboratory piece of this class will involve observations and experimentation in the urban environment of Boston. It will be our goal to understand how to transform city space through play, performance, networks and tools. The class will result in a design book and website that will function as a record of the knowledge produced during the semester with the intention of sharing widely.

assignments

Urban Augmentation (15%)
Urban spaces are composed of flows of people and things. There are patterns and habits that come to define every space. Using signage, screens and/or performance, create a change in how people occupy space. The augmentations should be documented on video and edited to demonstrate results.


Design Journal (25%)
Everyone will get a Tumblr for the course. The Tumblr should be used to keep reading notes, and design notes and updated at least once a week. These design journals will be graded twice during the semester.


Final Project Proposal (10%)
The final project proposal should be a thoughtful statement of a problem, complete with a theoretical justification. It should include a detailed solution statement that proposes the details of a project. This should include a literature review of similar projects, how they worked and what you intend to do differently.


Final Project (25%)
The final projects will be fully executed prototype projects. They will be critiqued by a panel and graded based on quality of execution and production values.


Final Project Documentation (25%)
All final projects will be documented via videos or images and included in the overall design site produced from the class.
